
# Section 3: People Management

## Q1: How do you manage your team

- Clarity  
  - I set clear goals, ownership, and expectations for my team of 14 engineers across two scrum teams  

- Execution  
  - I drive delivery through sprint planning, stand-ups, and regular tracking while removing blockers  

- People Management  
  - I conduct regular 1:1s, provide feedback, and support career growth  

- Balance  
  - I balance hands-on involvement with delegation, guiding when needed while empowering the team  

- Outcome  
  - This ensures strong delivery and a motivated, high-performing team  

---

## Q2: How do you evaluate team performance

- Dimensions  
  - I evaluate across delivery, quality, and behavior  

- Delivery  
  - Ownership and timely completion  

- Quality  
  - Code quality and production stability  

- Behavior  
  - Collaboration, initiative, and problem-solving  

- Differentiation  
  - Junior → execution, Senior → ownership, Architect → decision-making  

- Process  
  - Continuous feedback through 1:1s, not just periodic reviews  

---

## Q3: How do you identify and grow high performers

- Traits  
  - Take ownership beyond tasks, solve problems proactively, and deliver consistently with quality  

- Identification  
  - Based on delivery patterns, peer feedback, and initiative during critical situations  

- Validation  
  - I look for consistent behavior over time, not one-time performance  

- Growth  
  - I provide stretch opportunities, ownership, and leadership responsibilities  

- Outcome  
  - Helps scale strong contributors into future leaders  

---

## Q4: How do you handle low performers

- Identification  
  - Patterns like missed deadlines, quality issues, or lack of ownership  

- Diagnosis  
  - Identify root cause such as skill gap, clarity, or motivation  

- Action  
  - Provide clear feedback and structured improvement plan (PIP if needed)  

- Follow-up  
  - Regular check-ins and support to track improvement  

- Outcome  
  - Focus on helping them improve before taking strict actions  

---

## Q5: How do you build and motivate a high-performing team

- Clarity  
  - Define clear goals and expectations  

- Ownership  
  - Give team members ownership of work and decisions  

- Recognition  
  - Acknowledge contributions and achievements  

- Growth  
  - Provide learning opportunities and challenging problems  

- Alignment  
  - Connect work to business impact  

---

## Q6: How do you handle hiring and team building

- Requirement  
  - Define clear role expectations based on team needs  

- Evaluation  
  - Assess both technical skills and ownership mindset  

- Fit  
  - Ensure cultural and team fit  

- Outcome  
  - Hire people who can grow and contribute long-term  

---

## Q7: How do you mentor and develop team members

- Guidance  
  - Provide technical and career direction through regular 1:1s  

- Planning  
  - Define individual growth plans  

- Exposure  
  - Give real ownership and challenging work  

- Feedback  
  - Continuous and actionable feedback  

---

## Q8: How do you balance people management and technical involvement

- Focus  
  - Prioritize team, delivery, and outcomes  

- Involvement  
  - Stay engaged in architecture and key technical decisions  

- Delegation  
  - Delegate execution while focusing on direction and decisions  

---

## Q9: How do you handle team conflicts

- Understanding  
  - Listen to all perspectives  

- Evaluation  
  - Assess based on technical and business impact  

- Resolution  
  - Guide toward solution or validate via data/POC  

- Outcome  
  - Ensure decision benefits system and team  

---

## Q10: How do you handle pressure situations

- Structure  
  - Break problems into smaller parts  

- Prioritization  
  - Focus on critical items first  

- Communication  
  - Keep stakeholders and team aligned  

- Outcome  
  - Stay calm and guide team effectively during high-pressure situations
