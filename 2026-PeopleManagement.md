# Section 3: People Management

## Q1: How do you manage your team
- Clarity - Define clear ownership and sprint goals across two teams (14 engineers)  
- Accountability - Track sprint commitments vs actual delivery  
- Engagement - Use regular 1:1s to understand progress and challenges  
- Intervention - Step in when I see repeated spillovers or blockers  
- Outcome - Ensure predictable delivery and aligned team  

---

## Q2: How do you evaluate team performance
- Dimensions - Evaluate across delivery, quality, and behavior  
- Delivery - Track ownership and sprint commitment vs completion  
- Quality - Monitor defect leakage, rework, and production issues  
- Behavior - Assess collaboration, initiative, and incident ownership  
- Differentiation - Junior execution, Senior ownership, Architect decisions  
- Outcome - Balanced and data-backed performance evaluation  

---

## Q3: How do you identify and grow high performers
- Signals - Consistent delivery, low rework, strong ownership in critical situations  
- Identification - Look for patterns across multiple sprints  
- Validation - Use peer feedback and team impact  
- Growth - Assign critical modules, mentoring, and leadership roles  
- Outcome - Develop future leaders within the team  

---

## Q4: How do you handle low performers
- Identification - Repeated missed commitments, quality issues, low ownership  
- Diagnosis - Identify root cause (skill gap, clarity, motivation)  
- Action - Provide specific feedback with clear expectations  
- Tracking - Monitor improvement over next 1–2 sprints  
- Escalation - Move to structured plan (PIP) if no improvement  
- Outcome - Focus on improvement first, then accountability  

---

## Q5: How do you build and motivate a high-performing team
- Clarity - Define clear goals and expectations  
- Accountability - Track delivery and quality consistently  
- Recognition - Acknowledge strong contributions and ownership  
- Growth - Provide learning and leadership opportunities  
- Alignment - Connect work to business impact  
- Outcome - Build ownership-driven team culture  

---

## Q6: How do you handle hiring and team building
- Requirement - Define role expectations based on team gaps  
- Evaluation - Assess technical skills and ownership mindset  
- Fit - Ensure alignment with team culture and collaboration  
- Decision - Prioritize candidates with growth potential  
- Outcome - Build scalable and strong team  

---

## Q7: How do you mentor and develop team members
- Guidance - Provide direction through regular 1:1s  
- Planning - Define individual growth areas and goals  
- Exposure - Assign real ownership and challenging work  
- Feedback - Give continuous and actionable feedback  
- Outcome - Enable steady career growth  

---

## Q8: How do you balance people management and technical work
- Focus - Prioritize team outcomes and delivery  
- Involvement - Participate in key architecture and design decisions  
- Delegation - Delegate execution while guiding direction  
- Outcome - Maintain balance between leadership and technical depth  

---

## Q9: How do you handle team conflicts
- Understanding - Listen to all perspectives  
- Alignment - Align discussion to business and system impact  
- Decision - Evaluate trade-offs and guide resolution  
- Action - Use data or POC if needed  
- Outcome - Ensure balanced and effective decisions  

---

## Q10: How do you handle pressure situations
- Structure - Break problems into smaller parts  
- Prioritization - Focus on critical issues first  
- Communication - Keep team and stakeholders aligned  
- Execution - Handle immediate issues, then long-term fixes  
- Outcome - Maintain control and guide team effectively
