
# Section 9: Behavioral (Strengths / Failures)

## Q1: What are your key strengths

- Ownership  
  - I take end-to-end ownership of delivery, including planning, execution, and production stability  

- Problem Solving  
  - I stay calm during production issues and handle them in a structured, phased manner  

- Team Leadership  
  - I build strong teams through clear expectations, mentoring, and regular feedback  

- Technical Judgment  
  - I make balanced decisions across scalability, reliability, and maintainability  

- Business Alignment  
  - I align engineering work with business outcomes and customer impact  

---

## Q2: What are your weaknesses

- Depth vs Breadth  
  - Earlier, I used to go deep into technical details too quickly  

- Improvement  
  - Now I focus on high-level decisions first and dive deep only when required  

- Delegation  
  - I sometimes take on more responsibilities myself  

- Improvement  
  - I’m improving by delegating more and empowering team members with ownership  

---

## Q3: Tell me about a failure

- Situation  
  - During a platform migration, we underestimated system complexity and dependencies  

- Impact  
  - This led to delays in delivery timelines  

- Action  
  - I re-evaluated scope, improved estimation approach, and aligned stakeholders on revised plan  

- Learning  
  - Importance of validating assumptions early and planning dependencies in detail  

- Outcome  
  - Improved planning discipline in subsequent initiatives  

---

## Q4: How do you handle pressure situations

- Structure  
  - Break problems into smaller parts  

- Prioritization  
  - Focus on critical issues first  

- Communication  
  - Keep stakeholders and team aligned with clear updates  

- Execution  
  - Address immediate issues while planning long-term fixes  

- Outcome  
  - Helps maintain control and guide team effectively under pressure  

---

## Q5: How do you handle conflicts (team or stakeholders)

- Understanding  
  - I first understand all perspectives and underlying priorities  

- Alignment  
  - I align discussion to business goals rather than opinions  

- Decision  
  - I evaluate trade-offs like impact, timeline, and risk  

- Resolution  
  - I guide toward a balanced solution or phased approach  

- Example  
  - Balanced compliance-driven model upgrade with observability changes  

---

## Q6: How do you motivate and manage difficult team members

- Understanding  
  - Identify root cause such as skill gap, clarity, or motivation  

- Feedback  
  - Provide clear, specific, and constructive feedback  

- Support  
  - Offer guidance and structured improvement plan  

- Ownership  
  - Encourage accountability and involvement  

- Outcome  
  - Most individuals improve with the right support and clarity  

---

## Q7: What is your leadership style

- Approach  
  - Collaborative and outcome-driven  

- Decision Making  
  - Involve team in discussions but take final accountability  

- Focus  
  - Balance delivery, quality, and team growth  

- Outcome  
  - Builds trust, ownership, and consistent results
