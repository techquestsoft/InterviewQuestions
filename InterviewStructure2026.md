# Section 1: Introduction & Career

## Q1: Tell me about yourself

- Current Role  
  - I’m currently a Senior Engineering Manager at Oracle Cerner, leading Care Coordination platforms like Care Management and Readmission Prevention, which process large-scale patient data and support clinical decision-making across 100+ customers.

- Experience  
  - I have around 20 years of experience across banking and healthcare, where I’ve evolved from hands-on development to leading distributed systems and engineering teams.

- Key Achievement  
  - One of my key achievements was leading a platform modernization at Optum, where we migrated 100+ microservices from OpenShift to Kubernetes, reducing infrastructure costs by approx 5 million dollars annually.

- Current Responsibilities  
  - Currently, I manage a team of 14 engineers, drive roadmap execution, and lead key initiatives such as AWS to OCI migration and transitioning from rule-based models (V1/V2) to an ML-driven model (V3).

- Focus  
  - My focus is on delivering reliable systems and improving engineering efficiency aligned with business outcomes.

# Section 2: Delivery & Execution

## Q1: How do you manage delivery for your teams

- Planning  
  - At the beginning of each quarter, we participate in roadmap planning with product and architecture teams to define priorities.

- Breakdown  
  - This is followed by sprint planning and grooming where we break down features into actionable user stories with clear estimates.

- Execution  
  - During execution, I track progress through daily stand-ups, sprint dashboards, and regular check-ins.

- Risk Management  
  - I ensure risks are identified early and removed quickly so that delivery remains predictable.

---

## Q2: How do you ensure delivery predictability

- Planning Discipline  
  - We break down work into smaller, manageable user stories to avoid spillovers.

- Tracking  
  - We track sprint commitments versus actual delivery and identify gaps.

- Dependencies  
  - I ensure dependencies are identified early, especially across platform and architecture teams.

- Balance  
  - I balance feature work with tech debt and operational tasks to avoid last-minute surprises.

---

## Q3: How do you prioritize work

- Priority Order  
  - Production issues and customer impact come first.  
  - Next, committed product features.  
  - Then tech debt and platform changes.

- Risk-Based  
  - Security vulnerabilities and mandatory upgrades are prioritized.

- Example  
  - For example, when critical vulnerabilities like Log4j or CrowdStrike updates come, I align with product team and prioritize them even if it impacts feature delivery.

---

## Q4: How do you handle multiple initiatives

- Workstreams  
  - I separate work into clear workstreams.

- Ownership  
  - I assign ownership within the team.

- Tracking  
  - I track milestones and timelines regularly.

- Coordination  
  - Strong coordination with stakeholders ensures alignment and avoids delays.

---

## Q5: How do you handle delays

- Root Cause  
  - I identify whether the issue is estimation, dependency, or technical complexity.

- Communication  
  - I communicate early with stakeholders and provide revised timelines.

- Action  
  - I re-prioritize scope or adjust resources if needed.

- Learning  
  - I capture learnings to improve future planning and estimation.

---

## Q6: How do you manage sprint execution

- Preparation  
  - Sprint planning starts with grooming where user stories are refined and estimated.

- Execution  
  - I track progress through stand-ups and dashboards.

- Blockers  
  - I ensure blockers are resolved quickly.

- Retrospective  
  - At the end of the sprint, we review outcomes and identify improvement areas.

---

## Q7: How do you ensure stakeholder alignment

- Meetings  
  - Weekly feature and roadmap discussions with product teams.

- Forums  
  - Participation in CAB and architecture reviews.

- Outcome  
  - Ensures engineering, product, and platform alignment.

---

## Q8: How do you manage dependencies

- Identification  
  - Dependencies are identified during planning.

- Coordination  
  - Managed via scrum-of-scrums or direct communication.

- Tracking  
  - Continuous follow-up ensures progress.

- Escalation  
  - Early escalation if risk arises.

---

## Q9: How do you manage releases

- Governance  
  - Follow CAB, OHRM, and change management approvals.

- Validation  
  - Ensure proper testing and documentation.

- Deployment  
  - Controlled production rollout.

- Outcome  
  - Stable and compliant releases.

---

## Q10: How do you ensure quality

- Design  
  - Architecture and design reviews.

- Code  
  - Code reviews and standards enforcement.

- Improvement
