# 13A DATA CLASSIFICATION AND PROTECTION STANDARD

### 13A Data Classification and Protection 12.1
Description
It is the policy of QuestSoft and its Business Organizations (hereafter referred to as "Company" or "the Company") to use and disclose only as permitted under and in accordance with all applicable laws and regulations and, if also applicable, more strigent individual authorizations, customer contracts, and company polices.

This security standard applies:
	* Worldwide to data used and disclosed by or on behalf of the Company. However, when data is used or disclosed to or from locations outside of the United States, contract the Business Organization or appropriate Company Privacy Office for specific guidence.
	* To all Business Organizations with in the Company, including all current affiliates and subsidiaries, and immediately to all accquired affiliates and subsidiaries when incorporated into the Company; and
	* To all Company Group Workforce Members
	
For purposes of this Data Security Policy, "use and disclose" is general phrase that is interchangeable with any of the following actions; collect, access, modify, create, process, store, transmit, receive, destuct, or dispose; and the words "data" and "information" are used interchangeably.

Workforce or Workforce Members include Company employees and other non-employee workers such as third-party contractors, service professionals, and temporary staff. QuestSoft employees and non-employee workers will be collectively referred to as "Workforce" or "Workforce Member" although the use of the term does not establish a lobot relationship with the Company.

Company and Business Organization Privacy Policies contain addtional requirements regarding use and disclose of Protected information (as defined below).

For environments that are in scope for the Payment card Industry (PCI), please refer to 13D PCI Security for additional requirements.

### 13A.1 Data Classification
Area Statement
The Company will take reasonable precautions to secure Company data and data entrusted to the Company from unauthorized use or disclose. For these purposes, the Company classifies and labels data into one of three security levels and requires the information Owner to apply the proper classificaton level and lable.

### 13A.1.01 Data Classification levels
#### Statement
Company data, adn data entrusted to the Company by Members, customers, providers, suppliers, adn other third parties, should be identified and classified by the Information Owners as one of the following three data classificaton levels:
	1. Protected Informaton
	2. Confidential Information
	3. Public Information

These data classificaton levels are defined in 13A.1.01(A) bleow. Examples of data classificaton levels are given in 13A.1.01(B) below. Generally, decisions relating to whether a given item of information falls into one of the classificaton listed above will be made by the Privacy Office or Business counsel for the applicable business segment.

#### 13A.1.01(A) Classification Level Definitions
#### Statement
	1. Protected Information is data (in any format) that includes any of the following:
		i. Protected Health Information (PHI) or Electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI), as defined in the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPPA) and its implementing regulations. PHI or ePHI is individually indentifiable information that relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition of an individual; the provision of the health care to an individual; or the past, present, or future payment for the provision of the health care to an individual that is created, received, transmitted, or stored by a health plan, provider, or their supplier. PHI and ePHI includes any health information int he foregoing context used to identify an individual. Examples of data constituting PHI adn ePHI are given in 13A.1.01(B) below. Determinations of whether or not a given item of data constitues PHI or ePHI should be made by the Privacy Office for the applicable business segment.
		ii. Non-public Personal Information (NPPI), as defined under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA), any related state statutes, and any related federal or state regulations. NPPI is personally identifiable financial information that a financial institution (such as XXX Bank) collects about and individual in connection with providing a financial product or service, unless that information is otherwise publicly available. Examples of data constituting NPPI are given in 13A.1.01(B) below. Determinations of whether or not a given item of data constitues NPPI should be made by the Privacy Office for the applicable business segment.
		iii. Cardholder Data, as defined in teh Payment card Industry (PCI) Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS). Cardholder data and specific requirements relating to the storage and protection of Cardholder data are set forth in 13D PCI Security. Determinations of whether or not given item of data constitues Cardholder data udner hte PCI-DSS should be made by the Privacy Office for the applicable Business segment.
		iv. Personal Data/International Law, as defined in the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (the GDPR, which applies to all European Union (EU) Member states), the Brazillian General Data Protection Law ( the LGPD), and the Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2021 and implementation Rules and Regulations ( the DPA). Examples of Personal Data/International Law are given in 13A.1.01(B) below. Determinations of whether or not a given item of data constitues Personal data under International Law should be made by the Privacy Office for the applicable Business segment.
		v. Personally Identifiable information (PII), not regulated by HIPPPA, GLBA, PCI, GDPR, LGPD, or DPA but for which the disclosure and/or use is regulated by other applicable federal, state, or international laws. Examples of PII are given in 13A.1.01(B) below. Determinations of whether or not a given item of data constitues PII under the procted information classificaton should be made by the Privacy Office for the applicable Business segment.
		vi. Federal Tax Information (FTI), as defined under the Internal Revenue Code (IRC) 7213/7431/6103 protects the confidential relationship between the taxpayer and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS). Determinations of whether or not a given item of data constitues FIT should be made by the Privacy Office for the applicable Business segment.
		
	2. 	Confidential Information is data (in any format) that includes any of the following:
		
		i. Company Non-public Information is any information to which the Company owes a duty of confidentialily. Confidential Information may be in written, verbal or electronic form. This is information that if disclosed could subject the Company to loss of competitive advantage, loss of goodwill, a breach of contract cliam, or other damaging effect. Examples of Company Non-public Information are given in 13A.1.01(B) below.
		ii. Intellectual Property (IP) is a work or invention that is the result of creativity, of which the Company has rights and for which can apply for a patent, copyright, or trademark. IP also includes Trades Secrets, which are secret devices, techniques, or formulas used by the Company to provide goods and services.
		iii. Trade Secrets. See the Master Intellectual Property Policy - Concerning Patents, Trade Secrets, Copyrights, Trademarks, Domain Names, and Social Media (ID-5014).
		iv. Third Party Confidential Information is information of a third party that is subject to a non-disclosure agreement or other confidentiality obligations, including those in supplier, customer, and provider agreements. The terms of virtually all supplier, customer, and provider agre are third party Confidential information. Other examples of Third Party Confidential Information are given in 13A.1.01(B) below.
		
		Determinations of whether or not a given item of information constitues Confidential Information should be made by the Privacy Office or business counsel for the applicable business segment.
		
	3. Public Information is data (in any format) that is comprised of the following:
		
		i. Information relating to the Company that has been made publicly-available with appropriate permission from the Company. Examples of Public Information are given in 13A.1.01(B) below.
		
		ii. Third-party information that is not protected Information or Confidential information (as each of those terms is defined above).
		
		Determinations of whether or not a given item of data constitues Public Information should be made by the Privacy Office or business counsel for the applicable business segment.
