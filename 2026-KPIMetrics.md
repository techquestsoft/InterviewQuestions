# Section 7: KPI & Metrics

## Q1: How do you define and use KPIs for your team
- Dimensions - Define KPIs across delivery, quality, and business impact  
- Delivery - Track sprint predictability, on-time delivery, and commitment reliability  
- Quality - Monitor defect rates, production incidents, and system stability  
- Business Impact - Measure time to market, cost efficiency, and customer impact  
- Approach - Use balanced metrics and avoid relying on a single metric like velocity  
- Outcome - Ensure holistic performance aligned with business goals  

---

## Q2: How do you measure productivity and performance
- Outcome Focus - Focus on outcomes rather than activity metrics like story points  
- Delivery - Track consistency of delivery and ownership  
- Quality - Evaluate rework, defects, and production stability  
- Behavior - Assess collaboration, initiative, and problem-solving  
- Approach - Combine quantitative metrics with qualitative feedback from 1:1s  
- Outcome - Provide balanced view of productivity and performance  

---

## Q3: How do you measure ROI of engineering initiatives
- Cost Impact - Measure reduction in infrastructure and operational costs  
- Efficiency - Track improvement in delivery speed and reduced manual effort  
- Business Value - Evaluate faster releases and improved customer experience  
- Example - Platform modernization reduced infrastructure cost by ~ $5M annually  
- Outcome - Ensure engineering investments deliver measurable value  

---

## Q4: How do you prevent KPI misuse or gaming
- Balanced Metrics - Avoid reliance on single metrics like velocity  
- Cross-Validation - Combine delivery, quality, and business metrics  
- Trend Analysis - Evaluate performance over time, not one sprint  
- Outcome Focus - Prioritize real business outcomes over numbers  
- Outcome - Prevent metric manipulation and ensure meaningful insights  

---

## Q5: What KPIs do you track for system reliability
- Availability - Track uptime and service availability  
- Stability - Monitor production incidents and failure rates  
- Performance - Measure response times and system behavior under load  
- Trend Tracking - Analyze recurring issues and patterns  
- Outcome - Enable proactive reliability management  

---

## Q6: How do you align KPIs with business goals
- Alignment - Define KPIs based on product and business priorities  
- Mapping - Link engineering metrics to business outcomes  
- Example - Faster delivery leads to quicker releases and improved customer experience  
- Review - Regularly review KPIs with stakeholders  
- Outcome - Ensure engineering efforts drive business impact
