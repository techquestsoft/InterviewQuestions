
# Section 7: KPI & Metrics

## Q1: How do you define and use KPIs for your team

- Dimensions  
  - I define KPIs across three dimensions: delivery, quality, and business impact  

- Delivery  
  - Sprint predictability, on-time delivery, and commitment reliability  

- Quality  
  - Defect rates, production incidents, and system stability  

- Business Impact  
  - Time to market, cost efficiency, and customer impact  

- Approach  
  - I use a balanced set of metrics and avoid relying on a single metric like velocity  

- Outcome  
  - Ensures holistic view of team performance aligned with business goals  

---

## Q2: How do you measure productivity and performance

- Outcome Focus  
  - I focus on outcomes rather than activity metrics like story points  

- Delivery View  
  - Consistency of delivery and ownership  

- Quality View  
  - Rework, defects, and production stability  

- Behavior  
  - Collaboration, initiative, and problem-solving  

- Approach  
  - Combine quantitative metrics with qualitative feedback from 1:1s  

---

## Q3: How do you measure ROI of engineering initiatives

- Cost Impact  
  - Reduction in infrastructure or operational costs  

- Efficiency  
  - Improved delivery speed or reduced manual effort  

- Business Value  
  - Faster releases and improved customer experience  

- Example  
  - Platform modernization reduced infrastructure cost by ~ $5M annually  

- Outcome  
  - Ensures engineering investments deliver measurable value  

---

## Q4: How do you prevent KPI misuse or gaming

- Balanced Metrics  
  - Avoid relying on single metrics like velocity  

- Cross-Validation  
  - Combine delivery, quality, and business metrics  

- Trend Analysis  
  - Evaluate performance over time, not one sprint  

- Outcome Focus  
  - Prioritize real business outcomes over numbers  

- Outcome  
  - Prevents gaming and ensures meaningful measurement  

---

## Q5: What KPIs do you track for system reliability

- Availability  
  - Uptime and service availability  

- Stability  
  - Production incidents and failure rates  

- Performance  
  - Response times and system behavior under load  

- Trend Tracking  
  - Monitor patterns and recurring issues  

- Outcome  
  - Ensures proactive reliability management  

---

## Q6: How do you align KPIs with business goals

- Alignment  
  - Define KPIs based on product and business priorities  

- Mapping  
  - Link engineering metrics to business outcomes  

- Example  
  - Faster delivery → quicker releases → improved customer experience  

- Review  
  - Regularly review KPIs with stakeholders  

- Outcome  
  - Ensures engineering work directly supports business impact
